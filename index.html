Beautiful Landscapes

Japan is known for its stunning landscapes, including cherry blossoms in spring, lush green mountains, and serene temples.

Japan will amaze you with the diversity of its natural environment. The island stretches from north to south over 3000 kilometers; three quarters of it is mountainous, the rest consists of basins and plains. Japan has a mild climate and distinct seasonal changes. Because of its shape, the climate can vary greatly from place to place. For example, Hokkaido is known for its rigid and long winter, and ice drifts off its shores, while Okinawa is quiet, arid and belongs to the subtropical zone, with mangrove forests growing in many places. The island of Japan lies on four tectonic plates and it is the world's most geologically active zone. This is clear when observing the disposition of the islands, the many volcanoes, and hot springs welling up from underground. Volcanic activities also created the fuketsu, a cave where cold winds blow. Water is also ubiquitous: there are hundreds of rivers, deep lakes, and powerful waterfalls. Coastal areas are characterized by sandy beaches, bluffs, tidelands, and coral reefs. The sea surrounding the islands with warm and cold currents, is home to a huge variety of fish. There are about 6,000 islands, 430 of which are inhabited.

Cultural Richness

Experience the rich cultural heritage of Japan, from traditional tea ceremonies to vibrant festivals and historic landmarks.The culture of Japan has changed greatly over the millennia, from the country's prehistoric Jōmon period, to its contemporary modern culture, which absorbs influences from Asia and other regions of the world.

Japanese clothing

KIMONO:The kimono きもの/着物, is a traditional Japanese garment and the national dress of Japan.

MONTSUKI:Montsuki is a type of Japanese kimono that is characterized by the presence of a mon, or family emblem, on the fabric. The montsuki is usually worn by men on formal occasions and ceremonies, such as weddings, funerals and Japanese theater events.

Delicious Cuisine

Japan is renowned for its diverse and delectable cuisine. From traditional dishes to modern culinary creations, Japanese food has a unique and delightful flavor profile.

Sushi(寿司)

Sushi is perhaps the most famous Japanese dish internationally. It typically involves vinegared rice combined with various ingredients such as raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, and occasionally tropical fruits. There are different types of sushi, including nigiri, sashimi, and maki rolls.

Udon(うどん)

Udon (うどん or 饂飩) is a thick noodle made from wheat flour, used in Japanese cuisine. Udon noodles are boiled in a pot of hot water. Depending on the type of udon, the way it is served is different as well. Udon noodles are usually served chilled in the summer and hot in the winter.

Okonomiyaki(お好み焼き)

Okonomiyaki is a savory pancake made with flour, grated yam, eggs, shredded cabbage, and various toppings. It's often cooked on a hotplate and served with mayonnaise and okonomiyaki sauce.
